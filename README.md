3.Preceding Question Prompting

 Why is antibiotic resistance a global health crisis? Describe how overuse of antibiotics leads to superbugs

                  Antibiotic resistance is a global health crisis because it renders common infections untreatable, leading to millions of deaths annually and threatening modern medicine.
Scale of the Crisis
In 2019, bacterial resistance directly caused 1.27 million deaths worldwide and contributed to nearly 5 million more, with trends worsening as one in six infections now resists standard antibiotics. Resistance surged over 40% in key bacteria-drug combinations from 2018-2023, hitting hardest in regions like South-East Asia and Africa. Without intervention, it could cost trillions in GDP losses by 2030 and undermine surgeries, chemotherapy, and transplants.
How Overuse Creates Superbugs
Overuse and misuse of antibiotics expose bacteria to drugs repeatedly, killing susceptible strains while survivors—often with random mutations—thrive and multiply. These resistant "superbugs" pass genes to offspring or other bacteria via plasmids, rapidly spreading traits like resistance to last-resort drugs such as carbapenems. Agricultural and veterinary antibiotic use amplifies this selective pressure, turning routine infections into deadly ones.
